Section II
The "Singles Pentathlon" Rotation
⭐
The Rule
Over the 5 potential matches (3 Group Stage + 1 Semi + 1 Final), every member is scheduled to play exactly one Singles game.
🔒
Eligibility
Once you've done your Singles, you're "Doubles Only" for the rest of the tournament.
🎲
Luck of the Draw
Eliminated before all members get their turn? Those scheduled for later Singles simply won't get theirs.

Section III
Relay Scoring & The "Safety Cap"
💡 This league uses Continuous Relay scoring — points carry over between games. Think of it as one big race to 45!
🎯
Target Score
The match ends when a team reaches 45 points.
Game Transitions
Game Start Target Type
Game 1 0 – 0 15 Singles
Game 2 prev. 30 Doubles
Game 3 prev. 45 Doubles
Scenario — no cap triggered
  • Game 1: Team A leads, score reaches 15 – 10. Game 1 over.
  • Game 2: Starts at 15 – 10. New doubles pairs swap in. Play continues until a team hits 30. Score ends 28 – 30. Game 2 over.
  • Game 3: Starts at 28 – 30. Final doubles pairs. First to 45 wins the match!
The match is one continuous score — no resets between games.
🛡️
The 10-Point Safety Cap
If a game ends with a lead greater than 10, the trailing team's score is adjusted so no one starts more than 10 behind.
Example
Game 1 Ends 15 – 2
→
Game 2 Starts 15 – 5 ✓
Trail was 13 pts (>10) → adjusted to max 10-pt deficit
When does the cap kick in?
Only when the gap is greater than 10.
  • 15 – 5: Gap is 10 → ❌ No cap. Game 2 starts 15 – 5.
  • 15 – 4: Gap is 11 → ✅ Cap! Game 2 starts 15 – 5.
  • 15 – 0: Gap is 15 → ✅ Cap! Game 2 starts 15 – 5.
  • 30 – 16: Gap is 14 → ✅ Cap! Game 3 starts 30 – 20.
  • 30 – 22: Gap is 8 → ❌ No cap. Game 3 starts 30 – 22.
Formula: If (leader – trailer) > 10, set trailer = leader – 10.
⚡
No Deuce
First team to the target point wins the segment. No need for a 2-point lead.

Section IV
Match Conduct
🕐
Lineup Submission
Captains get 5 minutes before each match to submit their player sequence to the organizer.
🪶
The Toss
A shuttle toss determines Serve or Side at the start of the match.
↔️
Side Switching
Teams switch sides once per match — after Game 2 ends (when a team hits 30), before Game 3 begins.
Side switch happens after Game 2 ends (i.e., when the leading team reaches 30 points).

  • Game 1 (Singles): Play on initial sides.
  • Game 2 (Doubles): Same sides still.
  • 🔄 Switch sides now!
  • Game 3 (Doubles): Play on swapped sides.
This ensures fairness if there's a wind or lighting advantage on one side.

Section V
Standings & Tie-Breaking
📈 Ranking uses Total Cumulative Points scored across all group stage matches — not match wins. If teams are tied, the following tiebreakers apply in order:
💡 Every point matters! A team that loses 44–45 keeps those 44 points. Consistent, competitive play is rewarded even in a loss.
✅ 2-team tie? Head-to-Head always settles it — every match has a winner (first to 45, no draws).  3+ teams? A mini-league is formed (see below).
1
Head-to-Head
Winner of the direct match between tied teams.
2
Total Games Won
Most individual game wins (out of 9 in group stages).
3
Point Differential
Points scored minus points conceded.
4
Natural Sequence
Original registration/starting order.
Scenario: Teams A and B both end group stage with 120 cumulative points.

Step 1 — Head-to-Head: A beat B 45–38 in their direct match → Team A ranks higher. Done!

Since every match produces a winner (first to 45, no draws possible), head-to-head always resolves a 2-team tie. Tiebreakers 2–4 are never needed here.
How it works: When 3 or more teams are tied, form a mini-league using only the results among those tied teams. Then apply the tiebreaker cascade within this mini-league:

Example: Teams A, B, and C all tied on cumulative points. In their head-to-head matches: A beat B, B beat C, C beat A (circular!).

  • Step 1 — H2H (mini-league): Each team is 1-1 → tied → move on.
  • Step 2 — Games Won (mini-league): Count game segment wins only from A vs B, B vs C, and A vs C. If A won 4, B won 3, C won 2 → A ranks 1st, B 2nd, C 3rd.
  • Step 3 — Point Differential (mini-league): Net points from those matches only.
  • Step 4 — Natural Sequence: Registration order — always unique, always resolves.
💡 If any step partially resolves the tie (e.g., separates 1 team from a 3-way tie), the remaining tied teams restart from Step 1 as a smaller group.

Section VI
Injury & Sickness Protocol
⚠️ If a member can't play, the team still competes — remaining members cover extra games as needed.
🔀
Substitution
A substitute is chosen from the remaining members. That person covers the gap by playing two games in that match.
⚡
Singles Impact
If the absent player was scheduled for Singles, the substitute's appearance counts as the substitute's Singles turn for the tournament.
📉
Minimum Roster
3 players minimum to compete. Fewer than 3 available = match forfeit (can't field a Doubles pair).
🛡️
Singles Exception
If all remaining members have completed their Singles turn, an emergency exception is granted — any available member can play Singles without it counting as a second turn.
💚
Recovery
If the injured player recovers later, they should fulfill their Singles turn at the next available opportunity (best-effort, coordinated with the organizer).
Scenario: It's Match 2 and Dana is injured. Dana was scheduled for Singles this match.
  • The captain picks Bob to cover.
  • Bob plays Singles (Game 1) and one of the Doubles games.
  • This Singles game now counts as Bob's Singles turn for the tournament.
  • Dana should fulfill her Singles turn in a later match if the team advances and she recovers (best-effort).
🚨 If Bob had already played his Singles in Match 1, another eligible member must sub instead — you can't play Singles twice (unless all 4 remaining have already gone, in which case an emergency exception applies).
📉 Multiple absences? The same principle cascades. But if you're down to fewer than 3 players, the team forfeits — you can't field a Doubles pair.
